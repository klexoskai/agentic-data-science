# Data Science Pipeline Strategy

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the strategy for developing a lightweight end-to-end data science pipeline for iNova Pharma Pte Ltd, aimed at supporting new SKU launches in the FMCG/OTC healthcare sector. The pipeline will retrieve historical SKU launches similar to a given input and generate a 12-month projected sales trend based on historical performance data. This initiative is crucial for enhancing decision-making processes related to product launches and ensuring that insights are derived from reliable historical data.

## Problem Statement & Scope
The primary objective is to build a data pipeline that, given a hypothetical launch input consisting of `launch month`, `category`, `market`, and `brand`, will:
1. Retrieve up to three similar historical SKU launches.
2. Produce a simple 12-month projected sales trend based on the identified similar launches.

The scope is limited to using available internal CSV data, focusing on creating a reusable workflow for launch similarity and sales projection.

## Dataset Inventory & Data Quality Assessment
The following datasets will be utilized:
- **copa.csv**: Historical P&L actuals (PRODUCT_ID, PERIOD, COUNTRY_CODE, SALES_QUANTITY, REVENUE_GOODS).
- **pnl2425_volume_extracts_matched.csv**: FY24-25 P&L and volume forecasts (matched_SKU_ID, Market, forecast_volume_y1, forecast_net_sales_y1).
- **launch_tracker25_matched.csv**: SKU launch history (SKU Code, SKU Launch Month, Category, Market Specific, Brand).
- **tm1_qty_sales_pivot.csv**: Monthly actual quantity and net sales (sku_id, period, quantity, net_sales).
- **IQVIA_Asia_data1.csv**: Third-party Asia market size data (deferred for MVP).

**Data Quality Notes**:
- A canonical SKU mapping layer is required to standardize SKU identifiers across datasets.
- Month-level projections require strict period normalization to `YYYY-MM` for consistency.
- Data gaps and inconsistencies across datasets must be addressed during the cleaning process.

## Recommended Pipeline Architecture

### Phase 1: Data Preparation & Integration
1. **Data Ingestion**: Load all relevant datasets into pandas DataFrames.
2. **Data Cleaning & Transformation**:
   - Normalize SKU identifiers across datasets to create a canonical mapping.
   - Align date formats to `YYYY-MM` for consistency.

### Phase 2: Exploratory Analysis & Feature Engineering
1. **Historical Launch Similarity Retrieval**:
   - Implement a similarity scoring mechanism that combines categorical attributes (category, market) and temporal proximity (launch month) to retrieve up to three similar historical SKUs.
   - Use cosine similarity or a Jaccard index based on historical sales data to enhance retrieval accuracy.

### Phase 3: Modelling Approach
1. **Sales Trend Projection**:
   - Utilize Exponential Smoothing or ARIMA for sales projection based on historical sales data from the matched SKUs.
   - Validate model parameters using cross-validation to ensure robustness.

### Phase 4: Evaluation & Validation
1. **Output Generation**:
   - Produce outputs in the required formats (CSV, Markdown, HTML) and visualize the projections using Dash.
   - Ensure the narrative clearly articulates the logic and evidence behind the projections.

### Phase 5: Deployment & Monitoring
1. **Frontend Integration**: Create an interactive Dash app to facilitate user input and display results, ensuring that the app can regenerate outputs based on new inputs.
2. **Data Quality Assurance**: Implement validation checks to ensure data integrity and completeness throughout the pipeline.
3. **Stakeholder Engagement**: Establish a feedback loop with stakeholders to gather insights on the usability of outputs and incorporate their input into future iterations.

## Key Assumptions
- Historical sales trends are indicative of future performance for similar SKUs, although market dynamics may change.
- The canonical SKU mapping layer will be accurately created and validated.
- Similarity scoring will effectively retrieve relevant historical launches.

## Caveats & Limitations
- The reliance on historical data may not account for changes in market dynamics or consumer behavior since the last launches.
- The methodology for similarity scoring must be clearly defined to avoid retrieving irrelevant historical launches.
- Data quality issues may arise if the canonical mapping is not rigorously validated.
- The model's performance may be affected by overfitting if not properly validated.

## Open Questions & Prerequisites
- What specific metrics or KPIs do stakeholders prioritize when assessing the success of a new launch?
- Are there any additional constraints or preferences regarding the types of visualizations or reports to be generated?
- How frequently will the pipeline need to be updated with new data, and what is the expected volume of new launches to process?
- What external market data sources should be integrated in future iterations?

## Recommended Next Steps
1. Conduct a preliminary analysis of the datasets to assess data volume and completeness.
2. Develop the canonical SKU mapping layer and validate its accuracy.
3. Define the similarity scoring algorithm and ensure it is well-documented.
4. Implement the initial version of the pipeline, focusing on the outlined phases.
5. Engage stakeholders for feedback on the outputs and iterate based on their input.

This comprehensive strategy aims to create a robust MVP pipeline that meets the immediate needs of iNova Pharma while laying the groundwork for future enhancements.
